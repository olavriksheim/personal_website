Our Digital Age

For the Information technology course.

Competance Aims

  • Describe the different types of digital equipment, and explain the main features of how these work.
  • Explain how physical signals in computer equipment can be interpreted as binary numbers, character sets, graphic presentations, picture elements and sound.
  • Give an account of standards for communication between different forms of digital equipment, and between programs.
  • Give an account of the challenges and opportunities the digital world may bring to linguistic and cultural minorities.
  • Give an account of and argue for the necessity of rules and ethical norms in the use of information technology.
  • Describe, elaborate on and discuss information technology's potential and consequences.
  • Describe and suggest measures that can be taken against threats arising in the digital world.

Hacking

My group made a film about hacking, and where the border for what's acceptable should lay. Now that more and more information is digitalised, it's becoming more important to protect ths information. Therefore, malicious hackers are one of the biggest problems we face today. But not all hackers have malicious intents. Some have joined the battle against malicious hackers. We can categorise hackers into three categories: Black hats, White hats and Grey hats. Black hats are the typical hackers with malicious intents. They try to steal information, disable computer systems etc. White hats are hackers that try to improve security. They try to hack into systems, so they can fix eventual errors. Grey hats lie between Black- and White hats. They often try to help, but do it whitout permission. We can now ask ourselves, what these hackers shopuld be allowed to do. This is a very hard question, since each and every person has their own borders. We can therefore not answer where the border should lie.
